Who Was Frederic Tudor?

Born September 4 1783, Frederic Tudor was part of a rich family. Though he had a chance to go to Harvard at age 13, he instead chose to preocupy himself with business. During a visit to the Caribbean, he decided that he could make a fortune from selling ice there.

Early Life

Frederic Tudor was born September 4th 1783 into a rich family. Tudor’s father, William Tudor was a well known Boston lawyer. From a young age, Frederic had grown up in high society, and had enjoyed the upper class fashion of drinking iced drinks. In his 23th year, Frederic Tudor had the idea to ship ice cut from the ponds of Massachusetts.

Selling Ice

Tudor chose to ship his ice to the carribean, in hopes of selling ice to the masses. However, this turned out to be harder than he imagined. Although the people did enjoy the ice, the people in the caribean also had no experience in ice, thus they didn’t have any way of keeping it from melting. Without ice houses, all of Tudor’s profits quickly went down the drain.

Failure

After his failed attempt in the carribean, Tudor tried to sell his ice in Cuba, in which he lost even more money. He kept shipping ice, and kept learning new ways to save both ice and money. After spending parts of 1812 and 1813 in debitors prision due to being swindled by a buisness partner .Even after jail, Tudor was unabashed, he borrowed money to buy even more ice from massachusetts and critically, an icehouse in cuba. With this icehouse, Tudor was finally able to sell ice at a profit.

The Ice King

Soon, Frederic was expanding his buiness to Charleston, Savannah and New Orleans while also developing some new sales tactics. One strategy was to teach bartenders how to store and use ice in their cocktails, developing demand for his ice. Another was to teach coffee makers to use his ice in creating ice cream, creating for even higher demand. However, this total demand was soon outpacing his abiltiy to harvest ice. But a hotel owner named Nathaniel Wyeth came up with an invention that allowed horses to replace the lengthy hand cutting process that ice usually took. With a massive increase in his ice production, Tudor soon was selling ice from Hong Kong, to India, to the UK all while making a tidy profit. From his massive ice trade empire, he earned the nickname, “The Ice King”

The End of An Era

In 1864, Tudor’s death signaled a change in the american ice buisness, though his ice empire had stretched around the world and he is said to have amassed 11 million dollars (around 200 million today) it was not invincible to the progress of techology. In 1854 the advent of the commerical ice maker reduced the need for shipping american ice around the world. Yet this new technology created a new era, one of ice tongs, ice boxes. and refirdgeration.
